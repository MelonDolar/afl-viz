Section 01

The 2024 Season at a Glance

The AFL is Australia's most-watched domestic sporting competition. In 2024, 18 clubs across five states battled across 22 home-and-away rounds plus finals. Geelong and Brisbane Lions emerged as the powerhouses, while West Coast and North Melbourne struggled at the bottom.

Section 02

Where Do the Clubs Come From?

The AFL is not just a Victorian competition. Clubs are spread from Perth in the west to Brisbane in the north-east. The size of each dot reflects how many games the team won in 2024, and the colour shows which state they play in.

Chart 02 · Geographic Map
AFL Club Locations — Australia
Bubble size = 2024 wins. Colour = state. Hover a bubble to see details about the club, their home ground, and when they were founded.
Section 03

How Teams Scored and Conceded

The best teams score a lot and give up little. The scatter plot below maps every club's average points scored vs average points conceded. Teams in the top-left quadrant are vulnerable — they score little but leak big. Teams in the bottom-right are dominant.

Chart 03 · Scatter Plot
Points For vs Points Against
Each dot is a club. Dashed lines show the league average. Teams below and to the right of both lines are the competition's elite.

Section 04

Who Are the Stars?

AFL players fill different roles — midfielders win the ball through clearances and high disposals, forwards kick the goals that win games, and defenders rebuff attacks. Here we look at what separates the best from the rest.

Chart 04 · Lollipop Chart
Top Goal Scorers — Forwards Only
Only players classified as forwards with more than 20 season goals shown. Jeremy Cameron and Charlie Curnow led the competition.
Chart 06 · Bubble Chart
Midfielders: Disposals vs Brownlow Votes
Bubble size = average clearances per game. The Brownlow Medal is awarded to the fairest and best player, voted by umpires each match.
Chart 09 · Normalised Stacked Bar

How the mix of disposals, marks, tackles, clearances and inside-50s breaks down differently for defenders (DEF), forwards (FWD), midfielders (MID) and ruckmen (RUC).
Section 05

Crowd Passion Across the Nation

AFL draws enormous crowds, particularly in Victoria where the MCG holds over 100,000 people. Melbourne-based teams naturally attract the biggest attendances. The heatmap below shows crowd size by club and round — revealing when interest peaks.

Chart 05 · Heatmap
Average Crowd by Team and Round (Rounds 1–12)
Darker blue = larger crowds. Melbourne clubs in rounds involving MCG blockbusters (Collingwood vs Carlton, Essendon vs Melbourne) show the darkest cells.
Section 06

A Century of Premiers

The AFL (originally the VFL) has been running since 1897. Carlton, Collingwood and Essendon each share the record for most premierships with 16, though none have won recently. Geelong (10 flags) and Brisbane Lions (4) represent more modern dynasties.

Only teams with at least one premiership shown. Hover segments to see exact counts. The three oldest Melbourne clubs dominate all-time tallies.
